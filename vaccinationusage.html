BIRTH

B.C.G

Dose : 1 of 1

Protects against : Tuberculosis

To be given : Before discharge

How can you help protect your child?

BCG is currently the only available TB vaccine and can help protect against tuberculosis infection. The BCG vaccine can be provided as part of routine immunization for infants. Consult with your healthcare professional for further information.

O.P.V

Dose : 1 of 1

Protects against : Polio; a disease caused by a virus that can affect the nerves

To be given : As soon as possible after birth

How can you help protect your child?

The polio vaccine can be provided alone or included in combination vaccines that also help protect against other diseases, as part of routine immunization for infants. Unfortunately, there is no cure for the disease. Consult with your healthcare professional for further information.

How can you help protect your child?

The Hepatitis B vaccine can be provided alone or in combination vaccines that also help protect against other diseases, as part of routine immunization for infants. Consult with your healthcare professional for further information.

9 MONTHS

Measles-1

Dose : 1 of 2

Protects against : Measles, Mumps, and Rubella

To be given : At 9 months

How can you help protect your child?

Children should get two doses of measles vaccine, starting with the first dose at 9 months of age, and the second dose at 16 to 24 months of age. Teens and adults should also be up to date on their measles vaccination.

16-24 MONTHS

D.P.T Booster 1

Dose : 1 of 2

Protects against : Diphtheria, tetanus and pertussis. This vaccination is a booster that helps boost the immunity to the same diseases the DTP vaccine help protects from.

To be given : Between 16 to 24 months

How can you help protect your child?

The diphtheria, tetanus, and pertussis vaccinations are included in combination vaccines that also help protect against other diseases, as part of routine immunization for children.

Protection against Diphtheria People with diphtheria should be kept in isolation to stop the infection spreading; good hygiene practices are also recommended (e.g. hand washing and covering of mouths and noses when sneezing or coughing).

Protection against Tetanus You can only be protected from developing tetanus if you are vaccinated or if a treatment called tetanus immunoglobulin is injected after a serious injury.

Protection against Pertussis People with whooping cough should practice good hygiene (e.g. covering their/their infected child's mouth and nose when coughing and sneezing and washing their/their infected child's hands regularly) to reduce the spread of the disease

T.T Booster 1

Dose :1 of 2

Protects against : provide protection (immunity) against tetanus (lockjaw) in adults and children 7 years or older. Vaccination is the best way to protect against this life-threatening disease. Vaccines work by causing the body to produce its own protection (antibodies).

To be given : At 10 years

How can you help protect your child?

Td is only for children 7 years and older, adolescents, and adults.Td is usually given as a booster dose every 10 years, or after 5 years in the case of a severe or dirty wound or burn.
